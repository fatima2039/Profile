Interactive Art: 180 Seconds Drawing Challenge

  • C++
  • OpenFrameworks
  • Interactive Art

Overview:

Everyone loves a challenge and it can not ever get better when there is room for creativity in a challenge. With this idea in mind, I decided to make a drawing challenge OF app. The app user is given three minutes to recreate an image that is displayed. The images are pretty simple and drawn out of basic shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangles) originally. Learn more about it Here

The Approach:

The app will have a title screen, an instruction screen, and a 3-minute timer for the user to recreate a randomly chosen image. The app uses vectors to store information about the mouse location, brush size, shape color, color opacity, and brush number. The switching of screens is controlled by a global timer and the app uses the difference between the start time and current time to display texts and images at appropriate times. The font size and color of the timer changes during the last 10 seconds. Additionally, a background sound is played throughout the user's 3-minute session. Upon completion of the timer, the user's final progress is displayed and the mousePressed() and mouseDragged() functions no longer work.
