Game: The Presidential Race

  • Python
  • Processing
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Design

Overview:

This game is designed in a group of three as final project for Introduction to Computer Science course. The game is based around the theme of U.S 2020 Presidential Elections. The game has been implemented in Processing's Python mode to practice the Processing functions, Minmin Library and Python's OOP, conditionals and loops. Users can chose their charactor (Biden or Trump) and catch ballots to win the game.

Game Design:

The game is designed to provide options between two characters and three difficulty levels. Inorder to increase sscore, players need to move their character around the screen bottom using left and right arrow keys. The speed of the falling ballots is dependent on initial difficulty level and with increasing time, it increments also. All the ballots missed are added to opponents score and the player to first reach 270 points, wins the game. Additionally, there are some special ballots that have a higher score to them and these also increases player's speed across the screen. A slow down ballot decreases player's speed, making them lose more ballots.

This was my second experience with Processing , after the very basic Tetris Rush. Initially, it was very daunting, but then the team divided the design into smaller parts and worked on it. All these elements were constantly being enhanced. For this project, I worked on writing the code: designing the basic functioning of the game, design layout and all the information and selection pages on startup. Along with this, I was able to put my artsy skills in use by designing the ballots, and characters, backgrounds.
